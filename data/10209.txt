Adobe Patch Tuesday November Fixed Multiple Information Disclosure Vulnerabilities
This week, Adobe released its monthly scheduled update bundle addressing vulnerabilities within its different products. | PatchVulnerability
The Adobe patch Tuesday November updates allegedly fixed numerous vulnerabilities leading to information disclosure. | PatchVulnerability
These vulnerabilities existed in Adobe Acrobat/Reader, Flash Player, and Photoshop CC. | DiscoverVulnerability
The recently released Adobe Patch Tuesday November updates addressed three different vulnerabilities – all resulting in information disclosure. | PatchVulnerability
The first one existed in the Adobe Photoshop CC affecting versions 19.1.6 and prior for both Windows and MacOS. | DiscoverVulnerability
As described in the security advisory, Adobe has fixed this important Out-of-bounds read vulnerability (CVE-2018-15980) in the Photoshop CC versions 19.1.7 and 20.0. | PatchVulnerability
The second information disclosure flaw affected Adobe Reader and Acrobat for Windows. | DiscoverVulnerability
Explaining about the flaw in their advisory, Adobe stated, “Successful exploitation could lead to an inadvertent leak of the user’s hashed NTLM password.” | O
The vulnerability initially received the CVE number CVE-2018-4993, when Check Point Research first reported the bug. | DiscoverVulnerability
However, as recently disclosed by the EdgeSpot, Adobe only patched a single variant of this bug. | DiscoverVulnerability, PatchVulnerability
Whereas, the EdgeSpot team discovered other variants that hinted towards a failed patching of the bug instead of a new vulnerability. | DiscoverVulnerability, PatchVulnerability
The patched vulnerability has now received CVE number CVE-2018-15979 “to reflect that the patch is available”. | DiscoverVulnerability, PatchVulnerability
The third vulnerability addressed this month is an out-of-bounds Read vulnerability (CVE-2018-15978) in the Adobe Flash Player. | PatchVulnerability
The affected versions include 31.0.0.122 and earlier for Windows, Linux, and MacOS. | O
Unlike previous months, the Adobe Patch Tuesday November update bundle addressed fewer bugs. | PatchVulnerability
Moreover, none of the patched vulnerabilities had a critical severity impact. | O
In October, Adobe patched 86 different vulnerabilities including 47 critical ones. | PatchVulnerability
Whereas, in September, they addressed 6 critical flaws. | PatchVulnerability
Adobe has fixed the bugs CVE-2018-15980 and CVE-2018-15978 in Adobe Photoshop CC versions 19.1.7 and 20.0 and Adobe Flash Player version 31.0.0.148, respectively. | PatchVulnerability
Whereas, CVE-2018-15979 has received a patch in Adobe Acrobat DC and Reader DC version 2019.008.20081, Acrobat 2017 and Acrobat Reader DC 2017 version 2017.011.30106, and Acrobat DC and Acrobat Reader DC (Classic 2015) version 2015.006.30457. | PatchVulnerability
For protection against the three important vulnerabilities addressed in November updates, users should make sure to upgrade their software to the patched versions at the earliest convenience. | PatchVulnerability
