Security Researchers Urge The Guardian to Retract WhatsApp “Backdoor” Story | O
   Last week, we reported about a cryptographer who found a supposed vulnerability in WhatsApp and later gave an interview to The Guardian explaining the matter. | DiscoverVulnerability
The cryptographer later posted videos on YouTube explaining his findings, and WhatsApp’s response to his claims came in a timely manner. | DiscoverVulnerability
Cryptographer Tobias Boelter claimed that Facebook and WhatsApp could potentially intercept and read encrypted messages sent within the app. | DiscoverVulnerability
WhatsApp issued a statement saying that the alleged “backdoor” was actually an intentional design decision so that millions of messages could be delivered to their intended recipients. | DiscoverVulnerability
The developer of WhatsApp’s encryption protocol, Open Whisper Systems, also issued a statement backing up WhatsApp’s claims. | O
Now it seems that security researchers from around the world have come together to defend WhatsApp and urge The Guardian to retract its story. | O
