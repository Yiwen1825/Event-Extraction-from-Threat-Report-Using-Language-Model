Polish banks on alert after mystery malware found on computers | CSO Online
 
 

Polish media reported last week that the IT security teams at many Polish banks have been busy recently searching their systems for a particular strain of malware after several unnamed banks found it on their computers. | O
It's not clear what the malware's end goal is, but in at least one case it was used to exfiltrate data from a bank's computer to an external server. | AttackDatabreach
The nature of the stolen information could not be immediately determined because it was encrypted, Polish IT news blog Zaufana Trzecia Strona reported Friday. | O
After the malware program is downloaded and executed on a computer, it connects to remote servers and can be used to perform network reconnaissance, lateral movement and data exfiltration, the BadCyber researchers said in a blog post. | AttackDatabreach
The malware is similar to other crimeware tools, but has not been documented before. | O
