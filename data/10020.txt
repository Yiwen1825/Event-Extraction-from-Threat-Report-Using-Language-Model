Hackers Infect PGA Computers with Ransomware, Demand Bitcoin Payment | AttackRansom
PGA of America computers were infected this week with a strain of malicious software that locked down critical files and demanded cryptocurrency for their return. | AttackRansom
Officials discovered on Tuesday that servers had been targeted in a ransomware attack that  blocked them from obtaining access to material relating to major golf tournaments, including this week’s PGA Championship at Bellerive Country Club. | AttackRansom
Some signage had been in development for over a year and could not be reproduced quickly, Golfweek reported. | O
The extortion threat was clear: Transfer bitcoin to the hackers or lose the files forever. | AttackRansom
“Your network has been penetrated. |  All files on each host in the network have been encrypted with a strong algorythm (sic),” a ransom read. | O
“Backups were either encrypted or deleted or backup disks were formatted. | ” The note claimed shutting down the system may damage files. | O
The notice included a bitcoin wallet number—where funds could be sent—and a warning that there was no way to get access to the files without a decryption key. | O
The hackers that said they would prove their “honest intentions” to the PGA of America by unlocking two files free-of-charge. | O
A source who asked not to be named told Golfweek that officials had no intention of paying the ransom demand—following the advice of most law enforcement officials and cybersecurity experts. | AttackRansom
The network remained locked on Wednesday and external researchers are still investigating. | O
PGA of America has declined to comment. | O
The golfing association did not reveal what ransomware infected its computers. |  But tech website Bleeping Computer found the demand matched the BitPaymer variant. | O
Researcher Lawrence Abrams said one previous extortion scheme asked for 53 bitcoins, equivalent to $335,000. | AttackRansom
Abrams described BitPaymer as a “secure ransomware” and said the PGA would either have to rely on backups to regain access to its files or pay the significant bitcoin demand. | AttackRansom
